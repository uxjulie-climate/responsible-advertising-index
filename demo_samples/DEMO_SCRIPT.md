# RAI Demo Script & Presentation Guide

**Duration:** 15-20 minutes
**Audience:** Telekom stakeholders, potential partners
**Goal:** Show capability while being transparent about limitations

---

## Demo Flow

### 1. Introduction (2 minutes)

**Opening:**
> "Today I'll show you the Responsible Advertising Index - an AI-powered tool that analyzes advertising across four responsibility dimensions: Climate, Social, Cultural, and Ethical."

**Key Points:**
- Analyzes both images and video
- Provides scores 0-100 per dimension
- Identifies specific issues with evidence
- Currently in research/validation phase

**Set Expectations:**
> "Important note: This is a research tool providing directional insights. Scores aren't validated for absolute precision yet - we're conducting expert validation over the next 6 months. Think of it as a compass that points the right direction, not a ruler with millimeter precision."

---

### 2. System Overview (2 minutes)

**Show:** Main interface with tabs

**Point Out:**
- **Image Analysis** - Upload ad images
- **Video Analysis** - Upload video ads (NEW!)
- **Compare Ads** - Side-by-side comparison
- **Export Data** - PDF/Excel reports

**Mention:**
- Bilingual support (English/Hungarian)
- Built-in example ads for testing
- Real-time analysis in 10-60 seconds

---

### 3. Example 1: Excellent Ad (5 minutes)

**Select:** "Excellent: Sustainable Fashion" (EcoThreads)

**Ad Copy to Show:**
> "Repair Revolution - Every EcoThreads garment comes with lifetime repair guarantee. Since 2019, we've repaired 14,000 items. 100% organic cotton, GOTS certified. Fair Trade certified. Buy less, wear longer."

**Click:** "Analyze Ad"

**While Analyzing (10-15 seconds):**
> "The AI is analyzing both the visual elements and the text copy. It's looking for sustainability claims, diversity in representation, cultural sensitivity, and ethical communication practices."

**Results to Highlight:**

**Overall Score: 91/100**
> "This scores very high. Let's see why..."

**Radar Chart:**
- Climate: 95
- Social: 90
- Cultural: 88
- Ethical: 92

**Key Findings to Read:**
- **Climate:** "Specific, verifiable claims: '14,000 items repaired' - not vague greenwashing"
- **Social:** "Fair Trade certification indicates worker welfare consideration"
- **Ethical:** "Anti-consumerist messaging - 'Buy less' is counter to typical advertising"

**Talking Point:**
> "Notice how the AI identifies SPECIFIC evidence from the ad. It's not just giving a score - it's explaining WHY. This is what makes it useful: actionable insights, not just numbers."

**Show Strengths/Concerns/Recommendations:**
> "The system also provides recommendations. Even good ads can improve."

---

### 4. Example 2: Problematic Ad (5 minutes)

**Select:** "Problematic: Weight Loss" (SlimFit Pro)

**Ad Copy:**
> "Get Your Dream Body in Just 30 Days! Tired of feeling uncomfortable in your own skin? Drop pounds fast. Before: Unhappy. After: Unstoppable. Limited time: Buy 2, Get 1 FREE. Results may vary."

**Click:** "Analyze Ad"

**Results to Highlight:**

**Overall Score: 34/100**
> "This scores very low. Let's see what the AI caught..."

**Radar Chart:**
- Climate: 60 (neutral)
- Social: 15 (severe issues)
- Cultural: 40
- Ethical: 22 (major problems)

**Key Findings to Read:**
- **Social:** "Before/after imagery perpetuates body shaming and unrealistic standards"
- **Social:** "Messaging links self-worth to weight: 'uncomfortable in your own skin', 'feel beautiful'"
- **Ethical:** "Vague efficacy claims: 'Drop pounds fast' without specific evidence"
- **Ethical:** "Fine print disclaimer undermines main claims"

**Talking Point:**
> "The AI identifies multiple problematic patterns: body shaming, emotional manipulation, pressure tactics, and vague claims. This would be flagged for review before publishing."

**Show Recommendations:**
> "Notice the recommendations focus on wellness vs. appearance, eliminating manipulation, and providing actual evidence."

### 10. Transparency & Limitations (2 minutes)

**CRITICAL - Don't Skip This:**

> "Now, let me be transparent about limitations. This is important."

**Key Points:**

**1. Validation Status:**
> "We're in research phase. Scores are directionally accurate but not validated for absolute precision yet. We're conducting expert panel studies over the next 6 months."

**2. What It Is:**
- Research tool for insights
- Directional compass, not precise ruler
- Identifies issues for human review

**3. What It's NOT:**
- Not regulatory compliance tool
- Not replacement for human judgment
- Not ready for public brand rankings yet

**4. Use Cases:**
> "Best for: Internal assessment, tracking progress, identifying red flags, learning from best practices."

> "Not ready for: Public rankings, legal defense, competitive attacks."

**Show Document:**
> "We have comprehensive documentation on methodology, confidence levels, and validation strategy. Full transparency on what we know and don't know."

### 12. Questions & Discussion (Remaining time)

**Common Questions - Be Ready:**

**Q: "How accurate is it?"**
> "Direction: High confidence. Ranking: Medium. Exact scores: Being validated. We know it distinguishes good from bad ads reliably. We're validating the precise numbers with expert panels."

**Q: "Can we use this for compliance?"**
> "Not yet. It's a research tool that helps identify potential issues, but regulatory compliance requires legal review. Think of it as a first pass that flags things for expert review."

**Q: "What if it's wrong?"**
> "That's possible, which is why we require human review. We're transparent about limitations and tracking errors to improve accuracy. This is exactly why we're doing rigorous validation."

**Q: "How does it compare to manual review?"**
> "It's faster (seconds vs. hours) and more consistent (same framework always). But it's a complement, not a replacement. Best used together: AI for scale and consistency, humans for nuance and final decisions."

**Q: "Can we test our own ads?"**
> "Absolutely. That's the best way to evaluate if it's useful for you. We can set up a pilot with 50-100 of your ads and gather feedback."

## Backup Plan

**If Live Demo Fails:**
- Have screenshots ready
- Show the sample JSON reports
- Walk through the reports manually
- Emphasize the methodology

**If Questions Get Technical:**
- Refer to documentation
- Offer to connect with technical team
- Focus on use cases and value, not just tech

**If Someone Challenges Accuracy:**
- Acknowledge it's valid concern
- Show validation roadmap
- Explain transparency approach
- Offer pilot to gather feedback
